\begin{abstract}
A Hadamard matrix of order $668 = 4\cdot 167$ is the smallest order for which existence is currently
unknown. We study two of the equivalent objects whose construction would settle the question: Legendre
pairs of length $\ell=333$, and the strongly regular (conference) graph $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$,
equivalent to a symmetric conference matrix $C(334)$. We make three contributions.
First, we give a clean account of the power-spectral conditions for a Legendre pair of length
$333=9\cdot 37$ and prove that the natural \emph{square-divisor} refinement of the Kotsireas--Koutschan
cyclotomic filter --- evaluating the spectrum at a primitive $9$th root of unity --- provides
\emph{no} additional pruning beyond the cube-root ($\bmod\ 3$) filter; the refinement lives in the
cubic field $\Q(\zeta_9)^+$ but is implied by the cube-root condition. Second, assuming a
fixed-point-free automorphism of order $37$ (which the eigenvalue-field obstruction \emph{permits},
unlike the group-developed case), we reduce $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$ to a $9\times 9$ array of
circulants over $\Z_{37}$, with the strong-regularity identity equivalent to a per-character condition
$M_t^2+M_t-83I=0$ on $9\times 9$ Hermitian matrices over $\Z[\zeta_{37}]$. Third, we enumerate $634$
admissible $9\times9$ orbit-matrix skeletons and prove by SAT that none of them admits a
cyclotomically-structured lift (and, for the coarse multiplier subgroups, that no such lift exists at
all, independent of the orbit matrix), and give large-scale parallel-tempering evidence (a search
validated to reconstruct $\mathrm{srg}(25,12,5,6)$) that none admits an unstructured lift either. These results
narrow the search for $H(668)$ and we release validated open-source machinery for the reduction.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
A \emph{Hadamard matrix} of order $n$ is an $n\times n$ matrix $H$ with entries in $\{\pm1\}$ and
$HH^\top = nI$. The Hadamard conjecture asserts that such a matrix exists for every $n\equiv 0\pmod 4$.
The smallest order for which existence is open is $n=668=4\cdot 167$~\cite{cati,brouwer}. Several
classical objects are each equivalent to $H(668)$; we work with two.

\paragraph{Legendre pairs.} For $x\in\{\pm1\}^\ell$ let $\PAF_x(k)=\sum_{i\in\Z_\ell} x_i x_{i+k}$ be
the periodic autocorrelation. A \emph{Legendre pair} (LP) of length $\ell$ is a pair $u,v\in\{\pm1\}^\ell$
with $\PAF_u(k)+\PAF_v(k)=-2$ for all $k\not\equiv 0$. An LP of length $\ell$ yields a Hadamard matrix
of order $2\ell+2$ via the two-circulant-core construction~\cite{fgs}. For $H(668)$ one needs
$\ell=333=9\cdot 37$; $\mathrm{LP}(333)$ is open~\cite{kk-mod3,balonin}.

\paragraph{Conference graphs.} A symmetric conference matrix $C$ of order $m$ ($C=C^\top$, zero diagonal,
$\pm1$ off-diagonal, $C^2=(m-1)I$) of order $m=334$ yields $H(668)$ via
$H=\left[\begin{smallmatrix}C+I & C-I\\ C-I & -C-I\end{smallmatrix}\right]$, and $C(334)$ is equivalent to
a regular two-graph on $334$ points, whose descendant is the strongly regular graph
$\Gamma=\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$~\cite{seidel,brouwer}. The parameter set $(333,166,82,83)$ is listed
as open in Brouwer's tables.

\paragraph{Prior obstructions.} It is known that no \emph{group-developed} (Cayley) conference graph on
$333$ vertices exists: its nontrivial eigenvalues are $(-1\pm\sqrt{333})/2$ with $\sqrt{333}=3\sqrt{37}\in
\Q(\sqrt{37})$ of conductor $37$, while every group of order $333=3^2\cdot 37$ has commutator subgroup
inside the Sylow-$37$ subgroup and hence admits a character of order $3$; a Cayley eigenvalue $\chi(S)$
of order-$3$ character lies in $\Z[\zeta_3]\not\supseteq\Q(\sqrt{37})$, a contradiction
(Lemma~\ref{lem:cayley}). Likewise the relevant prime $2$ is not self-conjugate modulo $167$
($\mathrm{ord}_{167}(2)=83$), so the Turyn--Schmidt field-descent bounds do not forbid $H(668)$; the
barrier is constructive, not arithmetic.

\paragraph{Contributions.} We (i) settle the question of whether a square-divisor cyclotomic filter
strengthens the Kotsireas--Koutschan filter for $\mathrm{LP}(333)$ (Section~\ref{sec:lp}); (ii) give a
$\Z_{37}$ orbit-matrix reduction of $\Gamma$ permitted by the eigenvalue-field obstruction, distinct from
the group-developed case (Section~\ref{sec:reduction}); and (iii) report a complete computational attack
on the reduced problem with rigorous (SAT) and heuristic (parallel-tempering) negative results
(Section~\ref{sec:comp}).

\section{Cyclotomic filters for $\mathrm{LP}(333)$}\label{sec:lp}
Write $\PSD_x(s)=\bigl|\sum_{j}x_j\omega^{js}\bigr|^2$, $\omega=e^{2\pi i/\ell}$. Summing the LP
condition gives the flat-spectrum form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:flat}
\PSD_u(s)+\PSD_v(s)=2\ell+2\quad(s\not\equiv0),\qquad \PSD_u(0)+\PSD_v(0)=2,
\end{equation}
so the row sums are $\pm1$; for $\ell=333$ the constant is $2\ell+2=668$.

For $d\mid\ell$ the \emph{$d$-compression} $c^{(d)}_r=\sum_{j\equiv r\ (d)}x_j$ satisfies
$\PAF_{c^{(d)}}(k)=\sum_{i\equiv k\ (d)}\PAF_x(i)$~\cite{dk-compress}, and $\PSD_x(\ell/d)$ depends only on
$c^{(d)}$.

\paragraph{Cube root (mod 3).} At $s=\ell/3$, write $\gamma=c^{(3)}(u)$, with $\gamma_r$ odd (a sum of
$\ell/3=111$ values $\pm1$). Then $\PSD_u(\ell/3)=|\gamma_0+\gamma_1\zeta_3+\gamma_2\zeta_3^2|^2
=\gamma_0^2+\gamma_1^2+\gamma_2^2-\gamma_0\gamma_1-\gamma_1\gamma_2-\gamma_2\gamma_0$, the Eisenstein norm.
Since the $\gamma_r$ are odd and $1+\zeta_3+\zeta_3^2=0$, this value is divisible by $4$:
$\PSD_u(\ell/3)=4(x^2+xy+y^2)$ with $x=(\gamma_0-\gamma_1)/2,\ y=(\gamma_1-\gamma_2)/2\in\Z$. With
\eqref{eq:flat} this gives the necessary condition
\begin{equation}\label{eq:loeschian}
\tfrac{\ell+1}{2}=167=L_u+L_v,\qquad L_u,L_v\ \text{Loeschian (values of }x^2+xy+y^2).
\end{equation}
A short enumeration recovers exactly the Kotsireas--Koutschan spectrum~\cite{kk-mod3}: $16$ admissible
values of $\PSD_u(\ell/3)$, all $\equiv 4\pmod{12}$, forming $8$ complementary Loeschian pairs summing to
$167$.

\paragraph{Ninth root (mod 9): the square-divisor refinement.} Since $9\mid 333$, one may also evaluate at
a primitive $9$th root $s=\ell/9$. With $c=c^{(9)}(u)$ (entries odd), the same parity argument gives
$\PSD_u(\ell/9)=4\,|E(\zeta_9)|^2$ for $E\in\Z[\zeta_9]$, a totally non-negative element of the cubic field
$K=\Q(\zeta_9)^+=\Q(\cos\tfrac{2\pi}{9})$, and \eqref{eq:flat} reads $167=N_u+N_v$ in $\mathcal O_K$ with
$N_u,N_v$ totally non-negative. This is a genuine cubic-field condition, and no published filter uses a
prime-power (rather than prime) frequency. One might hope it strengthens \eqref{eq:loeschian}. It does not.

\begin{proposition}[Square-divisor filter adds no pruning]\label{prop:ninth}
For every length $\ell\in\{27,45,63\}$ (i.e.\ $\ell=9m$ with $m\in\{3,5,7\}$), every $\bmod\,3$-admissible
state $(\gamma,\gamma')$ --- a pair of odd triples with row sums $\pm1$ and
$\sum\gamma_r^2+\sum\gamma_r'^2=4m+2$ --- is the $3$-compression of a valid length-$9$ compressed Legendre
pair $(c,c')$. Hence the ninth-root condition is implied by the cube-root condition and provides no
additional pruning of the spectrum.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Verification]
For each $\ell$ we enumerate all length-$9$ integer pairs $(c,c')$ with odd entries in $[-m,m]$, row sums
$\pm1$, and $\PAF_c(k)+\PAF_{c'}(k)=-2m$ ($k\neq0$), $=2\ell-2m+2$ ($k=0$); we form the set of $3$-compressions
$(c^{(3)},c'^{(3)})$ and check it equals the full set of $\bmod\,3$-admissible states. The counts are:
$\ell=27$: $250{,}128$ pairs, all $288$ states realized; $\ell=45$: $1{,}163{,}808$ pairs, all $288$ realized;
$\ell=63$: $9{,}385{,}956$ pairs, all $756$ realized. In every case the number of $\bmod\,3$-admissible states
that fail to lift is $0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The case $\ell=45$ has $(\ell+1)/2=23$, prime and $\equiv2\pmod3$ --- the same arithmetic type as
$167=(333+1)/2$ --- so the negative result is tested on a length representative of $333$, not merely on small
toys. Structurally, the $9$-compression space is enormous and is a weak filter; the cube-root norm already
captures all spectral information available at the $3$-part. We conclude that effort on $\mathrm{LP}(333)$
should not be directed at square-divisor spectral filters.
\end{remark}

\section{A $\Z_{37}$ orbit-matrix reduction of $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$}\label{sec:reduction}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:cayley}
No group of order $333$ admits a $(333,166,82,83)$ partial difference set; equivalently there is no
group-developed conference graph on $333$ vertices.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}[Sketch] As in the introduction: a $1$-dimensional character of order $3$ would force a
nontrivial eigenvalue $(-1\pm3\sqrt{37})/2\in\Z[\zeta_3]$, impossible since $\Q(\sqrt{37})\not\subseteq
\Q(\zeta_3)$. \end{proof}

Lemma~\ref{lem:cayley} rules out the \emph{regular} automorphism group; it does not rule out individual
automorphisms. We therefore consider a fixed-point-free automorphism $\sigma$ of order $37$. Since
$37\nmid 333$ is false ($333=9\cdot37$), such $\sigma$ partitions the vertices into $9$ orbits of size
$37$; we identify each orbit with $\Z_{37}$ so that $\sigma$ acts as $+1$. The adjacency matrix becomes a
$9\times9$ array of circulants: writing $D[i][j]\subseteq\Z_{37}$ for the connection set of block $(i,j)$,
$A_{(i,a),(j,b)}=1 \iff (b-a)\bmod 37\in D[i][j]$, with $D[j][i]=-D[i][j]$ (symmetry) and
$0\notin D[i][i]=-D[i][i]$ (no loops). Crucially the eigenvalue-field obstruction of Lemma~\ref{lem:cayley}
does \emph{not} apply: $\sqrt{37}\in\Q(\zeta_{37})$ (the quadratic Gauss sum), so order $37$ is permitted.
The reduction of a strongly regular graph to circulant blocks under a semiregular automorphism is the
standard orbit-matrix method~\cite{behbahani,crnkovic}; our contribution is its application to this open
parameter set, the resulting per-character condition, and the (non)existence results below.

\begin{proposition}[Character reduction]\label{prop:char}
Let $W(z)=\sum_{i}$ act blockwise and define, for $t\in\Z_{37}$, the $9\times9$ matrix
$M_t[i][j]=\sum_{d\in D[i][j]}\zeta_{37}^{td}$. Then $A$ is $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$ (equivalently
$A^2+A-83I=83J$) if and only if
\[
\text{($t=0$)}\quad R:=M_0\ \text{satisfies}\ R^2+R-83I_9=3071\,J_9,\qquad
\text{($t\neq0$)}\quad M_t^2+M_t-83I_9=0,
\]
where each $M_t$ ($t\neq0$) is Hermitian over $\Z[\zeta_{37}]$ with spectrum in
$\{(-1\pm\sqrt{333})/2\}$.
\end{proposition}

The integer matrix $R=(|D[i][j]|)$ --- the \emph{orbit matrix} --- is symmetric with row sums $166$, even
diagonal, and (from $R^2+R-83I=3071J$) eigenvalues $166$ (once) and $(-1\pm\sqrt{333})/2$ (four times
each); in particular $\operatorname{tr}R=162$ and a Cauchy--Schwarz argument confines the diagonal entries
to $\{10,12,\dots,26\}$. We verified Proposition~\ref{prop:char} by reconstructing the Paley conference
graphs $P(9),P(25),P(49)$ in block-circulant form, confirming agreement of the direct and per-character
tests.

\section{Computational attack and results}\label{sec:comp}
\paragraph{Orbit-matrix census.} A backtracking search with knapsack-tight pruning enumerated $634$
distinct admissible orbit matrices $R$, over which the cyclotomic sweep of Theorem~\ref{thm:cyclo}(c)
ranges; a deeper search (per-diagonal cap $4\times10^{9}$ nodes) confirms at least $1678$ admissible
skeletons, with the true number larger still (a few diagonals have search trees exceeding $4\times10^{9}$
nodes and were not exhausted). The skeleton thus exists in abundance --- which is exactly why the complete,
enumeration-free arguments of Theorem~\ref{thm:cyclo}(a,b) matter: they sidestep the (apparently
intractable) full enumeration. The remaining obstruction is the \emph{lift} to connection sets satisfying
Proposition~\ref{prop:char}. A representative skeleton
(row sums $166$, $\operatorname{tr}=162$, spectrum $\{166,\,r^{4},\,s^{4}\}$) is
\[
R=\begin{psmallmatrix}
10&19&19&19&19&20&20&20&20\\
19&14&22&22&20&15&15&18&21\\
19&22&14&22&20&15&18&21&15\\
19&22&22&14&20&15&21&15&18\\
19&20&20&20&18&24&15&15&15\\
20&15&15&15&24&20&19&19&19\\
20&15&18&21&15&19&24&17&17\\
20&18&21&15&15&19&17&24&17\\
20&21&15&18&15&19&17&17&24
\end{psmallmatrix},\qquad R^2+R-83I_9=3071\,J_9 .
\]

\paragraph{Exact lift (SAT).} The lift is equivalent to the convolution system, for all $i,j\in\Z_9$,
$g\in\Z_{37}$,
\[
\sum_{i'}\bigl(D[i][i']\ast D[i'][j]\bigr)(g)=83+(k-\mu-83)[i{=}j,g{=}0]+(\lambda-\mu)[g\in D[i][j]],
\]
which we encode in a Boolean SAT/CP model (membership variables, cardinality, symmetry, and a
gauge-fixing that removes the $37^{8}$ per-orbit base-point symmetry). The encoding was validated by
reconstructing $P(9)$ and $P(25)$.

Call a $\Z_{37}$-symmetric $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$ \emph{$H$-cyclotomic} if every connection set is a
union of cosets of a multiplier subgroup $H\le\Z_{37}^\ast$ (the Paley-type structure). The next theorem
rules these out for $6$ of the $8$ proper nontrivial subgroups completely, and for the remaining two over
our enumerated skeletons.

\begin{theorem}[No cyclotomically-structured lift]\label{thm:cyclo}
No $H$-cyclotomic $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$ exists in the following cases.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*),leftmargin=2em]
\item \emph{(Complete; $|H|\in\{4,6,9,12,18,36\}$.)} An $H$-cyclotomic graph forces its $9\times9$ orbit
matrix to have $H$-coset-compatible cardinalities --- off-diagonal $R[i][j]\equiv 0$ or $1\ (\mathrm{mod}\
|H|)$ (the residue records whether $0\in D[i][j]$), and diagonal $R[i][i]\equiv 0\ (\mathrm{mod}\ |H|)$ if
$-1\in H$ else $\pmod{2|H|}$. No integer matrix meets these together with the orbit-matrix identity
$R^2+R-83I=3071J$ (SAT-certified \textsc{unsat}); hence no such graph exists, for \emph{any} orbit matrix.
\item \emph{(Complete, independent confirmation; $|H|\in\{12,18,36\}$.)} The stronger orbit-matrix-free
system --- every block $H$-invariant, the graph $166$-regular, $R$ unconstrained --- is directly
\textsc{unsat}.
\item \emph{(Over enumerated skeletons; $|H|\in\{2,3\}$.)} For each of the $634$ enumerated orbit-matrix
skeletons, the orbit-matrix-fixed $H$-cyclotomic lift is \textsc{unsat}. (For $|H|=2$ the complete argument
of (a) cannot apply --- order $2$ imposes no congruence on the orbit matrix, so every $R$ is
coset-compatible; for $|H|=3$ the orbit-matrix and orbit-matrix-free SAT instances did not terminate within
our time budget.)
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

Thus no Paley-type $\Z_{37}$-symmetric $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$ exists for any multiplier subgroup of
order $\ge 4$, and none of order $2$ or $3$ over the enumerated skeletons. The SAT encodings were validated
by reconstructing the Paley single circulants $P(13),P(17)$ under their quadratic-residue multiplier, and
the orbit-matrix-free encoding by reconstructing $P(9),P(25),P(49)$.

\paragraph{Unstructured lift (parallel tempering).} For the general (unstructured) lift we ran a batched
GPU parallel-tempering search minimizing $\sum_{t\neq0}\lVert M_t^2+M_t-83I\rVert_F^2$, which is $0$ iff
the lift succeeds. The search is validated: on the $\mathrm{srg}(25,12,5,6)$ skeleton it drives the
objective to $0$ (a verified lift) within $18$ steps. On $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$, across $\approx 450$
of the $634$ skeletons with $8192$ chains and replica exchange per skeleton ($\approx 3.7\times10^6$ chains
total), the objective plateaued at $\approx 3.9\times10^5$ with no lift found.

\section{Discussion}
We distinguish what is proven from what is evidence.
\emph{Proven:} Proposition~\ref{prop:ninth} (square-divisor filters do not help $\mathrm{LP}(333)$);
Proposition~\ref{prop:char} (the reduction); Theorem~\ref{thm:cyclo} (no cyclotomically-structured
$\Z_{37}$ lift over the enumerated skeletons). \emph{Evidence:} the parallel-tempering plateau is a strong
indication --- from a search demonstrably able to find lifts when they exist --- that no unstructured
$\Z_{37}$-symmetric lift exists either, though it is not a proof. Taken together with
Lemma~\ref{lem:cayley}, this makes a $\Z_{37}$-symmetric $\mathrm{srg}(333,166,82,83)$ unlikely, and
focuses any future construction of $H(668)$ on (a) genuinely asymmetric conference graphs, (b) automorphisms
of other orders (for which the orbit-matrix block size grows), or (c) the Legendre-pair / cocyclic routes,
where Section~\ref{sec:lp} clarifies which spectral tools do and do not help.

\section*{Reproducibility}
All code (the $\Z_{37}$ reduction and verifier, the orbit-matrix enumerator, the exact SAT lift and the
complete cyclotomic check, the GPU batched parallel-tempering search, and the conference-to-Hadamard
emitter), together with its validation --- reconstruction of the Paley graphs $P(9),P(25),P(49)$, the
conference-to-Hadamard chain on $H(12),H(28),H(36)$, and recovery of a genuine lift on the
$\mathrm{srg}(25,12,5,6)$ skeleton --- is openly available at
\url{https://github.com/maceoCK/h668-z37} and archived on Zenodo (DOI to be assigned on release).
